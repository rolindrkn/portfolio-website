Hello. I'm Rosalind, a life long learner, currently in software development.

How did I get here?

I was a very curious kid growing up. I loved opening up wrist watches or box radios to see how things ticked. I may not have restored the items complete with all the screws but I was fascinated how things worked. I never knew this was what I wanted to do until later in life. Being a girl, this was not the acceptable Asian career path as I was encouraged to become a Doctor or Lawyer. I just knew I was mechanically inclined.

My Best Friend

Around 2013, my best friend Curious (my cat) died of Feline Leukemia. I wanted to learn what happened and wondered if I could have done something to save her. With this determination, I changed my major to Animal Science.

Animal Science

About a year later, I was going through the motions of waking up, going to university class and biding my time to graduate. My life wasn’t very interesting. However, my direction in life changed once I worked on my Capstone Project and wrote my Thesis paper. After an exhaustive search, I finally found a Professor willing to work with me (the girl who was just complacent to be there). Surprisingly, I enjoyed researching and writing the paper. I learned so much about Animal Science writing my paper than I did in my whole University career.

That was it!!!! I wanted to be a researcher. Next step was to do a Masters degree. It was rewarding producing questions and answering them through research. Unfortunately, I was not able to get enter the Masters degree program so I applied for a related job in my field.

Startup company

I was lucky enough to learn about a startup company working on a topic in my Thesis manuscript. Eager to be part of their team, I applied for the “Technician” position. However, the job was in British Colombia and I just completed my degree in Saskatchewan. Luckily, I was offered the position and I packed up all my belongings and headed to BC for my new career.

When I started the Technician job, I learned that I was a Production Technician. “What was I supposed to do?” I had blindly taken a job without knowing what it entailed. I just had to make the best of the situation as a lab position will surely come up one day. I was wrong, but staying was the best decision I made.

Being with the company, I learned many skills and I learned specifically what I liked and disliked in a job. I excelled in:

  • machine operations
  • troubleshooting
  • production optimiaztion

By my 3rd month, I was promoted to Assistant Lead Hand. What I loved most about the job was the learning and mastering the job processes. I also started helping out in other department and this kept me going to work everyday. When machinery stopped working, I stayed with the maintenance team to learn and study how they resolved the issue. With my supervisor’s permission, I found how to optimize processes and spent more time learning.

Eventually, my learning plateaued and it was harder for me to wake up for work. So I started asking myself. “What am I doing this for?” I wanted to do more. So I came up with two options. I could stay with the company and move up or I could go back to school and study technology since this is what I feel is my best fit.

I chose the latter. Which brings me here. Do I regret my choice? Sometimes I wonder what would have happened if I didn’t switch fields. But then I wouldn’t have learned about the IT field. I don’t know if anyone would ever be certain where they are going or what they want to do, but if you don’t try it, you will never know.

Thank you for reading about my journey, I wish you luck with yours.
